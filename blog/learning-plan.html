To infinity and beyond

18 Feb '22

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

I don’t want to be setting very specific job title/role goals. I am in this after not using my degree. My plan is to gain entry into a tech role and explore the sphere of possibilities from there. It would be good to start talking to people about what avenues there are though so I can target entry-level roles more specifically. Either way, I want to continue to grow my skills so that I have some security. Not the security of the same job for an extended period, but the security of knowing that my skills are still relevant and in demand.

A description of your own strengths and limitations in relation to learning.

Strengths:

  • I have a great curiosity for technical stuff.
  • I can keep truckin’ when I hit a snag.
  • I enjoy the material.
  • I can lose hours as I am so focused on the problem.
Limitations:
  • When I do hit a snag, I often feel quite stupid. I keep going but feel stupid for taking too long.
  • I get fixated on a first solution.
  • I struggle to pause and follow a problem-solving framework.

  • Non-violent communication skills. Please google this if you are ??
  • How to get extremely clear on a team about who is responsible for what.
  • When and how to hold another person accountable.
  • Time-management.
  • Networking.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme.

  • I am fully committed time-wise and have cleared all conflicts. I will allocate some time to maintaining my relationships with my partner and key friends.
  • I have started implementing many of the suggested learning habits. Meditating, goal setting, reflection. This combined with my time commitment will hopefully see me through.
  • I will conduct myself with compassion and professionalism around all co-learners, educators and other community members.
  • When things start getting to me I will ask for help to prioritise what is essential and also to get a fresh perspective on how I might divide the problems.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way.

I will develop relationships with my co-learners, asking for help either in person or on slack. If the problem is bigger, unsolvable or not appropriate for them I will reach out to a facilitator or tutor through those same channels.

A description of what you expect from the facilitation team

  • Honest qualitative feedback.
  • Suggestions on where I personally might stretch my competencies and where my knowledge and skill gaps are.
  • Perspective on what's important.
  • Hold me to a high standard. That is a big one for me.
